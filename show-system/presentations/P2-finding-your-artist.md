### Slide 9: Deposits & Scheduling

**Visual:**
- Calendar with booking timeline
- Deposit payment example
- Cancellation policy graphic

**The Booking Process:**

**Step 1: Consultation**
- In-person or virtual
- Discuss design, placement, sizing
- Get quote and timeline

**Step 2: Design Approval**
- Artist creates custom design
- You review and request changes
- Final approval before appointment

**Step 3: Deposit**
- Typically $100-$200 or 50% of total
- Holds your appointment
- Often non-refundable

**Step 4: Appointment**
- Arrive on time, well-rested
- Bring ID and payment
- Follow pre-tattoo instructions

**Typical Timelines:**
- Popular artists: 3-12 month wait
- Mid-level artists: 1-3 month wait
- Newer artists: 1-4 weeks

**Speaker Notes:**
```
Once you've chosen your artist, here's what to expect:

You'll have a consultation‚Äîeither in person or virtual. You'll discuss your idea,
placement, sizing. They'll give you a quote and timeline.

Then they'll create a custom design. You'll review it, request tweaks, approve it.

Next: deposit. Most artists require $100-$200 or 50% of the total cost to hold
your spot. This is usually non-refundable if you cancel.

Then you wait. Popular artists might have a 3-12 month waitlist. Mid-level artists
might be 1-3 months out.

On the day of your appointment: show up on time, well-rested, hydrated. Bring
your ID and payment. Follow any pre-tattoo instructions they gave you.

Sarah, what surprised you most about the booking process when you started?

[Sarah shares booking insights]

Patience pays off. Don't rush this.

### Slide 10: Booking Mistakes to Avoid

**Visual:**
- Mistake scenarios illustrated
- Wrong way vs. right way comparisons
- Warning signs

**Common Mistakes:**

**‚ùå MISTAKE #1: Price Shopping**
- Asking 20 artists "how much for a sleeve?"
- Going with the cheapest quote
- FIX: Choose based on portfolio fit, not price

**‚ùå MISTAKE #2: Pinterest Copying**
- Bringing a Pinterest tattoo: "I want exactly this"
- Every artist does their own interpretation
- FIX: Bring inspiration, not replication demands

**‚ùå MISTAKE #3: Rushing the Process**
- Booking the first available artist
- Getting tattooed while drunk/impaired
- FIX: Research, wait for the right artist

**‚ùå MISTAKE #4: Ignoring Red Flags**
- "They seemed sketchy but the price was good"
- Ignoring dirty studios or rude behavior
- FIX: Trust your instincts, walk away

**‚ùå MISTAKE #5: No Backup Plan**
- Putting all hope in one artist
- Not researching alternatives
- FIX: Have 2-3 artist options

**Speaker Notes:**
```
Before we wrap up, let's cover the biggest mistakes people make when booking:

MISTAKE #1: Price shopping. Messaging 20 artists asking "how much for a sleeve?"

MISTAKE #2: Pinterest copying. Bringing a Pinterest screenshot and saying "I want
